# Electric Scouts - Link Building & Authority Growth Strategy

**Objective:** Systematically acquire high-quality backlinks to increase domain authority, improve keyword rankings, and drive referral traffic.

---

## Core Principles

1.  **Quality over Quantity:** Focus on acquiring links from relevant, authoritative websites in the finance, real estate, and energy sectors.
2.  **Relevance is Key:** Pursue links from content related to moving, personal finance, home improvement, and local state/city news.
3.  **Anchor Text Diversity:** Use a mix of anchor text, including target keywords, branded terms ("Electric Scouts"), and generic phrases ("click here").

---

## High-Priority Link Building Tactics (Months 1-3)

### 1. Guest Posting

*   **Description:** Write and publish high-quality articles on other websites in exchange for a backlink.
*   **Target Audience:** Personal finance blogs, real estate blogs (especially for movers), and home improvement sites.
*   **Process:**
    1.  Identify 50-100 target blogs with Domain Authority (DA) of 20-50.
    2.  Pitch relevant article ideas (e.g., "5 Ways to Lower Your Utility Bills After a Move," "The Ultimate Financial Checklist for New Homeowners").
    3.  Write a 1,500-word, non-promotional article that provides genuine value.
    4.  Include one contextual link to a relevant Electric Scouts article or tool.
*   **Goal:** 3-5 high-quality guest post links per month.

### 2. HARO (Help a Reporter Out)

*   **Description:** Respond to queries from journalists and bloggers looking for expert sources on energy, savings, and utilities.
*   **Process:**
    1.  Monitor HARO emails daily for relevant queries.
    2.  Provide concise, expert-level responses to queries about electricity markets, energy savings, or utility bills.
    3.  Include a brief bio mentioning Electric Scouts.
*   **Goal:** 1-2 high-authority media links per month.

### 3. Broken Link Building

*   **Description:** Find broken links on other websites and suggest a relevant Electric Scouts page as a replacement.
*   **Process:**
    1.  Use tools like Ahrefs or SEMrush to find broken outbound links on competitor sites or industry resource pages.
    2.  Identify broken links pointing to outdated energy guides, defunct providers, or 404 pages.
    3.  Contact the site owner, inform them of the broken link, and suggest a relevant Electric Scouts article or page as a replacement.
*   **Goal:** 2-3 links per month.

---

## Medium-Priority Link Building Tactics (Months 3+)

### 4. Local Business Directory Listings

*   **Description:** Create listings for Electric Scouts on local and national business directories.
*   **Process:**
    1.  Submit the business to directories like Yelp, Yellow Pages, and local chambers of commerce for major cities (Houston, Dallas, Chicago).
    2.  Ensure NAP (Name, Address, Phone) consistency, though as an online business, the focus is on the website link.
*   **Goal:** 5-10 directory links per month initially.

### 5. Infographic & Data Visualization Outreach

*   **Description:** Create compelling infographics (e.g., "The True Cost of Electricity in Each State") and promote them to bloggers and journalists.
*   **Process:**
    1.  Create a visually appealing infographic with unique data.
    2.  Write a blog post hosting the infographic.
    3.  Reach out to 50+ relevant websites, offering the infographic for them to use on their site in exchange for a link.
*   **Goal:** 2-4 links per created infographic.
